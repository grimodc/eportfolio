    • Sport & Other Activities

    Sport has always been an essential part of my personal balance. It helps me stay motivated, focused, and disciplined — qualities I also apply in my studies and projects.

    Personal Sports Activities

    Cycling

    I regularly go cycling to discover countryside and landscapes. It’s both a physical challenge and a way to clear my mind.

    Running

    I started running regularly only a year ago. Before that, I used to run occasionally, without a clear routine. Since I’ve made it part of my weekly schedule, I’ve learned to better control myself, stay consistent, and understand the importance of discipline — because running is a demanding sport where real progress comes only through patience and perseverance.

    Sports at ENSEEIHT

    Semester 5 - Run & Fit

    At ENSEEIHT, I’m part of a sports module called Run & Fit , which combines running and cross-training. At first, it felt quite similar to what I was already doing on my own but it turned out to be a whole new experience. Training with friends from school, sharing the effort and the good mood every Friday morning, gave the activity a real team spirit. It’s also the first time I’ve worked with a professional coach, and it’s very different from high school PE classes : more structured, more demanding, and much more rewarding.

    Analyzing performance through data

    Tracking my sport activities on Strava or Relive has strengthened my curiosity for data analysis and mapping tools. I enjoy observing how metrics such as speed, altitude, and time can be visualized to show progression, but also how they can be exploited to create activity maps (Strava) or dynamic videos (Relive). This analytical mindset is closely related to my interest in data science and applied mathematics, bridging my studies and my hobbies.
