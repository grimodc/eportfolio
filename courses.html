    Final Bachelor degree project

    citation lorenz

    Together with three other students, we conducted an in-depth study of the Lorenz Dynamical System , a set of nonlinear ordinary differential equations known for its chaotic behavior.

    Our work combined both theoretical analysis and numerical experimentation: we first explored the mathematical properties of the system — stability, sensitivity to initial conditions, and attractors — before implementing numerical methods to visualize and analyze its chaotic trajectories.

    This project allowed us to deepen our understanding of nonlinear dynamics, ODE solvers, and scientific computation using tools such as Python and Matplotlib.

    Presentation about Radon Transform

    As part of a mathematics and imaging course, I gave a 10mn-presentation on the Radon Transform, a fundamental mathematical tool used in computed tomography. The goal was to explain how this transform allows the reconstruction of an image from its projections, as done in medical scanners. I introduced the underlying mathematical concepts — integration over lines, inverse transform, and reconstruction algorithms — and illustrated them with practical visualization example. This work provided a concrete link between theory and real-world applications in medical imaging and inverse problems.
